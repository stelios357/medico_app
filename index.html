The reality

Every morning is
a gauntlet.

You're a PG1. It's 7 AM. Before you've had tea, your JR2 arrives, then the SR, then two consultants — one of them very awake, very particular. Each one asks what you did for your patient and why.

The knowledge is there. The protocol is somewhere in a 40-page IAP PDF you opened on Google three nights ago. The dose is different for every child's weight. You do this math multiple times a shift.

No one taught you the steps. Your seniors teach experience. The bookish language is yours to figure out. You're in a new hospital, clueless about the PICU protocols. You manage 7–8 dengue cases, 3 pneumonias, a febrile seizure, and a neonatal jaundice — all in one duty.

And when rounds are over, there's no place to decompress. No one to tell. You carry it home.
